Apple's first venture into spatial computing, the Vision Pro, was a technical marvel with a major problem: it was heavy and cost $3,499. But according to supply chain leaks from Taiwan and Shenzhen, Apple is preparing to resolve these pain points with the **Apple Vision Pro 2**, slated for a late 2026 launch.

## The Weight Dilemma Solved

The biggest complaint about the first-generation headset was its physical comfort. Users reported neck fatigue after an hour of use.

Leaked CAD drawings show that Apple has relocated parts of the glass front panel and replaced the heavy aluminum enclosure with durable, lightweight composites. These structural modifications are rumored to reduce the overall weight from 650 grams down to **under 450 grams**—a 30% reduction that should make extended sessions far more comfortable.

## The Price Cut We Need

To drive mainstream adoption, Apple is reportedly splitting the Vision lineup into two tiers:
1. **Apple Vision (Standard)**: Retailing around **$1,500 - $1,800**, this model will use slightly lower-resolution displays and fewer external cameras, making spatial computing accessible to a broader audience.
2. **Apple Vision Pro 2**: Retailing around **$3,000**, retaining the ultra-premium dual-micro-OLED displays and introducing the next-generation M5 processor.

With these updates, Apple hopes to cement VisionOS as the default computing interface for the next decade. If these leaks prove accurate, spatial computing could finally enter the mainstream market.
